Literature

Poetry Readings in Palace Coffee

April 18 – 2:00 – 8:00 p.m.

Calling All Poets! Sign up for a 10-minute spot at Palace Coffee to read your original poetry. Theme and style aren’t limited, but please remember that poems need to be appropriate for general audiences. If you have any questions about the poetry reading event, please email aafpoetryreadings@gmail.com If you’re ready to grab a spot, go here and sign up for a time with your name and a contact number. There may be slots available to claim during ArtsFest.

Enjoy poetry? Come and be a part of the audience—enjoy the entertainment and support our local poets.

Six Stories in 60 Minutes

April 18 – 1:00 p.m.

Local authors Seth Wieck, Chris Hudson, Mike Akins, Ryan McSwain, Jenny Stalter, and Nancy Keel read their flash fiction stories.

What Do You Need in Your Writing Toolbox?

April 18 – 4:00. p.m.

Local authors Ryan McSwain, Rick Treon, and Kim Black will be the panelists. This event will be held in the Badger Den on the second floor of the College Union Building (above Palace Coffee).

The Art of Dialogue

April 18 – 6:00 p.m.

Local authors Mike Akins, Wayne Hughes, and Suzi Sandoval will be the panelists. This event will be held in the Badger Den on the second floor of the College Union Building (above Palace Coffee).

Visual Interpretation of Poetry Contest

In celebration of Texas High Plains Writers’ centennial, we are having a contest!

Three local poets have each written a poem for an artist to create a piece of art to pair with!

There will be a 1st and 2nd place for each poem with a cash prize of $100 and $50, respectively, and will be displayed at ArtsFest 2020.

Judges are members of the ArtsFests’ Literary Committee. Entries are due April 2nd.

If you are interested in submitting your artwork interpretation of any of the poems, please email a photo of and the dimensions of your artwork as well as your cell phone number to info@texashighplainswriters.com.

The Poems

  • Highway 60 by Matt Morgan
  • Turbulence by Wes Reeves
  • Tornado Alley by Chera Hammons

Visual Arts

If you are interested in submitting your work of art for the ArtsFest visual arts galleries, please send an email to AmarilloArtsFestVisual@gmail.com with the following information:
  • Email subject: Gallery Artist
  • Picture of piece
  • Your name and phone number where we may reach you
  • Art Title
  • Description of piece
  • Dimensions
  • Contact email/phone number for clients
  • Name of business if applicable

Submissions close March 17th.
Pieces must be 5 feet or less in height and width.
Feel free to submit more than one painting/sculpture. We may select more than one depending on space available by jury date.
Drop offs will be at Amarillo College between March 21st and April 4th.
