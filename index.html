Literature

Poetry Readings in Palace Coffee

April 18 – 2:00 – 8:00 p.m.

Calling All Poets! Sign up for a 10-minute spot at Palace Coffee to read your original poetry. Theme and style aren’t limited, but please remember that poems need to be appropriate for general audiences. If you have any questions about the poetry reading event, please email aafpoetryreadings@gmail.com If you’re ready to grab a spot, go here and sign up for a time with your name and a contact number. There may be slots available to claim during ArtsFest.

Enjoy poetry? Come and be a part of the audience—enjoy the entertainment and support our local poets.

Visual Interpretation of Poetry Contest

In celebration of Texas High Plains Writers’ centennial, we are having a contest!

Three local poets have each written a poem for an artist to create a piece of art to pair with!

There will be a 1st and 2nd place for each poem with a cash prize of $100 and $50, respectively, and will be displayed at ArtsFest 2020.

Judges are members of the ArtsFests’ Literary Committee. Entries are due April 2nd.

If you are interested in submitting your artwork interpretation of any of the poems, please email a photo of and the dimensions of your artwork as well as your cell phone number to info@texashighplainswriters.com.

Visual Arts

Visual Arts has a building to itself where we will have a gallery on the 2nd and 3rd floors in the Parcells Building on Amarillo College Campus (Washington).

The 2nd floor will showcase various styles of paintings, and sculptures, while the 3rd floor will feature local photographers’ works, and a photography class hosted by Renee West. Auburn Adams will also offer a painting class during the day on campus in a separate building. Times for this class will be available at www.amarilloartsfest.com soon.

Outside, just north of the Parcells Building, we will have booth spaces for local artisans to share their talents with people passing through all the days events.
